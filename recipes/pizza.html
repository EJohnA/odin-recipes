Homemade Pepperoni Pizza

This Pepperoni Pizza recipe produces a quick and easy classic! Delicious homemade pizza crust and tomato sauce have never been easier.

Ingredients:

For the Sauce:

  • 1/2 Cup of Water
  • 1/2 (12 Ounces) Tomato Paste - This recipe recomends Contadina brand paste but anything will work. Consider ingredients already in your store-bough paste, and adjust added ingredients accordingly.
  • 1 teaspoon Dried, Crushed Oregano.
  • 1 teaspoon Dried, Crushed Basil.
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garlic Powder.
  • 1/2 teaspoon Onion Powder.
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sugar.
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt.
  • 1/4 teaspoon Black pepper.

For the Crust:

  • 3 1/4 cups All Purpose Flour.
  • 2 (.25 ounce) envelopes of Pizza Yeast, or Rapid Rise yeast - This recipe recommends Fleischmanns.
  • 1 tablespoon Sugar.
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon Salt.
  • 1 1/3 cups very warm Water - 120F - 130F.
  • 1/3 cup Oil.

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup Shredded Mozzarella Cheese.
  • 6 ounces Pepperoni.

Directions:

  1. Preheat Oven and Prepare Pans - Preheat to 425 Degrees F and apply a film of oil to two 12 inch pizza pans.
  2. Prepare the Sauce - Whisk together Water, Tomato Paste, Oregano, Basil, Garlic Powder, Onion Powder, Sugar, Salt and Pepper in a medium bown until smooth. Set aside.
  3. Make the Crust - Combine 2 cups Flour, Yeast, Sugar and Salt in a large bowl. Add warm water and oil. Mix until well blended (should take about 1 minute). Gradually add remaining Flour until a soft, sticky dough forms.
  4. Transfer Dough to a Floured surface - Knead until smooth and elastic (should take about 4 minutes). Add more flour as needed. If using Rapid Rise Yeast, let Dough rest covered for 10 minutes.
  5. Divide Dough in half. Apply a light amount of flower to your hands, and gently pat each piece of Dough onto your pans, forming the shape as you go.
  6. Top the Dough with Sauce, Cheese and Pepperoni.
  7. Bake in the preheated over until Crusts are browned and Cheese is bubbly. Expect 18-30 minutes cook time. Rotate pans between top and bottom racks halfway through.
